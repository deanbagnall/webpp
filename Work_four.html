George Marshall Medical Museum

Rebrand

"Both stylish and medically simplistic."

Dean Bagnall | 01-12-2014

George Marshall Medical Museum holds a collection of Worcester's medical history. The collection spans 250 years of history, and shows the advancements in those years. The rebrand had to emcompass the museum's ability to deliver an experience that shows the timeline of medical history. The logo itself must be contempary but be able to stand the test of time like many of the artifacts on display. With this rebrand having both medical and historical roots it was important for me to ensure the logo was both stylish and medically simplistic. The dichotomy between the objects made me hestitant to use any museum piece within my logo, with a second trepidation of favourtism towards a partical artifact.

Through various visual mock-ups and ideas the logo ended up being a carefully considered and crafted. The four piece rectangle set was concieved that housed the letters GMMM. The logo was them accomopanied by a strapline of George Marshall Medical Museum. The strapline was added to ensure the logo mark by itself wasn't used as a primary option, to ovoid confusion and enhance recognition of the brand. The typeface used was Helvetica Neue, a solid typeface that has zero character and acts purely objectivly. This was to ensure maximum readability & legibility while also being tied into a classic, timeless medically used typeface.

Overall the project was a welcomed challenge and proved to be a exciting and rewarding experience.
